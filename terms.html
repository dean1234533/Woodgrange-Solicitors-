1. Our Firm

Woodgrange Solicitors LLP is a limited liability partnership registered in England and Wales, authorised and regulated by the Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA No. 573793). Our registered office is at 46 Woodgrange Road, Forest Gate, London E7 0QH.

These terms of business ("Terms") govern the relationship between Woodgrange Solicitors LLP and our clients. By instructing us, you agree to be bound by these Terms.

2. Instructions & Engagement

Our engagement begins when we send you a client care letter or engagement letter confirming your instructions. That letter will set out the scope of work, our fee estimate, and the key contact handling your matter.

We will act only on the instructions of the named client(s). We may decline to accept instructions or cease acting in accordance with our professional obligations or these Terms.

We will communicate with you primarily by email unless you request otherwise. You are responsible for providing us with accurate and up-to-date contact information.

3. Fees & Payments

Our fees are calculated by reference to the time spent on your matter, at our standard hourly rates, which will be set out in your client care letter. We may also charge fixed fees for certain services.

We will provide you with a cost estimate at the outset and update you if it appears costs will exceed the estimate. Invoices are due within 14 days of issue.

We are entitled to charge interest at the rate of 8% per annum on unpaid invoices after the due date. We may also exercise a lien over your papers and documents until our outstanding fees are paid.

Disbursements (such as court fees, Land Registry fees, or counsel's fees) are payable in addition to our professional fees and will be invoiced as incurred.

4. No Win No Fee

No Win No Fee arrangements (Conditional Fee Agreements, or CFAs) are available for certain categories of work at our discretion. Where a CFA is agreed, the specific terms — including the success fee percentage and circumstances under which fees become payable — will be set out in a separate written agreement signed by both parties.

Not all matters are suitable for CFAs. We will assess eligibility during your initial consultation.

5. Confidentiality

We owe you a duty of confidentiality. All information relating to your matter is kept strictly confidential, subject to exceptions required by law (e.g. disclosure to regulatory authorities, court orders, or our anti-money laundering obligations).

We maintain secure electronic and physical systems to protect your information. Please refer to our Privacy Policy for full details.

6. Anti-Money Laundering

We are required by the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 to verify the identity of all clients before commencing work. We will ask for identity documents (such as a passport and proof of address) and may use electronic verification services.

We may also be required to make a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) to the National Crime Agency without notifying you if we become aware of information that gives rise to such a suspicion. This is a legal obligation and does not affect our duty of confidentiality in other respects.

7. Limitation of Liability

We maintain professional indemnity insurance as required by the SRA. Our aggregate liability to you in connection with any matter is limited to the amount recoverable under our professional indemnity insurance, which is a minimum of £2 million per claim.

We are not liable for losses arising from circumstances outside our reasonable control, or from inaccurate or incomplete information provided by you.

8. Termination

You may terminate our engagement at any time by giving written notice. You will be responsible for our fees and disbursements incurred up to the date of termination.

We may cease to act for you if: you fail to give us clear instructions; there is a conflict of interest; you fail to pay our invoices; continuing to act would breach our professional obligations; or there is a breakdown in the relationship. We will give you reasonable notice and ensure your matter is not prejudiced.

9. Governing Law

These Terms and any dispute arising out of or in connection with them are governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
