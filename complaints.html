1. Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a high quality legal service to all our clients. If something goes wrong, we need you to tell us about it. This will help us to improve our standards and put things right where we can.

We take all complaints seriously and aim to handle them promptly, fairly, and confidentially.

2. How to Complain

If you have a complaint, please contact our Complaints Partner in the first instance. You can do this:

  • In writing: Woodgrange Solicitors LLP, 46 Woodgrange Road, Forest Gate, London E7 0QH (marked "Private & Confidential — Complaints")
  • By email: complaints@woodrangesolicitors.co.uk
  • By telephone: 020 8534 2400 and ask to speak to the Complaints Partner

Please provide your name, matter reference number, a clear description of your complaint, and the outcome you are seeking.

3. What Happens Next

  1. Acknowledgement: We will send you a written acknowledgement of your complaint within 3 business days of receiving it.
  2. Investigation: Your complaint will be reviewed by a senior partner who was not directly involved in your matter. We may contact you to clarify details.
  3. Response: We will send you a full written response within 8 weeks of receiving your complaint. This will explain our findings and, where appropriate, any proposed remedy or redress.

Remedies we may offer include an explanation, an apology, a reduction in fees, or compensation for direct financial loss where appropriate.

4. Timeframes

Stage Timeframe
Acknowledgement of complaint Within 3 business days
Full investigation and response Within 8 weeks
Escalation to Legal Ombudsman (if unresolved) After 8 weeks or receipt of our final response

5. If You Remain Unsatisfied

If you are not satisfied with our response, or if we have not resolved your complaint within 8 weeks, you have the right to complain to the Legal Ombudsman, an independent complaints body for the legal sector.

You should contact the Legal Ombudsman within one year of the act or omission you are complaining about, or within one year of when you should reasonably have known there was cause for complaint.
